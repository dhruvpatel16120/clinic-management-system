# Five-Minute Demo Runbook

Use this exact story during the first clinic demos. Do not wander across every screen.

## Demo Goal

Show one clean patient journey:

1. front desk books the visit
2. queue moves the patient to the doctor
3. doctor creates the prescription
4. reception closes billing and payment

If the clinic can picture this working at their desk, the demo has done its job.

## Demo Flow by Screen

### Minute 0 to 1

Start in receptionist workflow:

- show appointments
- create or open the patient visit
- mention that walk-ins and booked patients can both be handled

Say:

> This is where your front desk stops juggling registers and WhatsApp notes. The patient is captured once and the visit moves through the clinic from here.

### Minute 1 to 2

Move to token queue:

- issue or show the live token
- explain that the waiting line is visible without shouting patient names repeatedly

Say:

> The queue gives your front desk and doctor the same live picture of who is waiting and who is next.

### Minute 2 to 3

Switch to doctor workflow:

- open the visit
- create the prescription
- point out follow-up and medicine entry

Say:

> The doctor does not need to re-create patient context. The visit moves from reception into the consultation workflow directly.

### Minute 3 to 4

Return to billing:

- create invoice
- mark payment
- show printable invoice or payment history

Say:

> Once the consultation is done, billing stays attached to the visit. That means fewer missed payments and a cleaner day-end record.

### Minute 4 to 5

Close the workflow:

- summarize the journey
- ask whether this is closer to how their clinic actually runs
- offer the pilot

Say:

> If this matches your clinic flow, I can set up a 14-day pilot, onboard the owner and front desk, and keep the rollout tight enough that you know very quickly whether it is worth adopting.

## Screens to Avoid in the First Demo

Do not lead with:

- legal pages
- admin edge cases
- every settings field
- every report
- technical explanation of Firebase or permissions

The clinic is buying smoother operations, not architecture.

## What a Good Demo Should Achieve

By the end of 5 minutes, the clinic should understand:

- the front desk has one place to start the patient journey
- the doctor sees the same patient flow
- prescriptions and billing do not live in separate chaos
- the pilot is guided, not dumped on the clinic

## Demo Close

Use this exact close:

> If you want, I can set this up as a 14-day pilot for your clinic and onboard the owner plus front desk first. That will let your team test the workflow with real daily use before you commit to a monthly subscription.
